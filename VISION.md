# Vision

## The Problem

Vibe coding — delegating all technical thinking to AI — is creating cognitive atrophy in developers. It's not a knowledge problem. It's that the path of least resistance is always the prompt. The technical muscle atrophies when you stop using it, the same way it happens with calculators or GPS.

The modern developer faces a paradox: companies reward the speed of vibe coding, but technical agency — reading code, making architecture decisions, reasoning under pressure — is still what separates a senior from someone who only knows how to prompt.

There's a term for this: **delegated cognitive atrophy**. And like any atrophy, the fix is intentional exercise.

## What Dojo Solves

Dojo creates **intentional friction**. The path of least resistance here is the hard path — thinking for yourself, working through discomfort, submitting something imperfect. That's the point.

It's not about preparing for interviews. It's about staying technically alive in a world where the pressure is to delegate everything.

## The Philosophy

**Process over correctness.** The sensei doesn't evaluate whether you found the optimal solution. It evaluates whether you reasoned well, identified the right tradeoffs, and understood what you were building. A working solution written badly is more interesting than a perfect solution you can't explain.

**Variability is a feature.** Two developers with the same exercise base may have different experiences depending on the variation generated. That's fine. This is a gym, not a certification. Consistency of practice matters more than consistency of grading.

**Vulnerability with humor.** The share card that says "the sensei told me a junior with 6 months experience would have written this cleaner 💀" — that's the tone. Honest about failure, light about it, proud of showing up anyway.

**The dojo doesn't babysit.** No pause button on the timer. No reroll on the kata selection. No AI during the exercise. The system trusts you to honor the practice. If you cheat yourself here, that's between you and your career.

## Who It's For

Developers who feel the pull of vibe coding and are worried about what it's doing to their ability to think independently. Developers who want to stay sharp without abandoning the tools that make them fast. Developers who can laugh at their own gaps while actively closing them.

Initially: the creator (pure dogfooding).
Then: invited developers who feel the same pull.
Eventually: any developer who wants to maintain technical agency.

## What Success Looks Like

Not a large user base. Not viral growth. Success is a small group of developers who use the dojo consistently, who feel the difference in how they approach problems, and who contribute kata back to the pool because they found value in the practice.

The best outcome: someone uses the dojo for 6 months and realizes they stopped reaching for the AI prompt as the first instinct. They reach for their own reasoning first, then use AI to validate or extend it. That's the shift.

## What Dojo Is Not

It is not a replacement for AI tools. It is not anti-AI. It is the thing you do so that when you use AI, you're directing it rather than following it.

The analogy: a chef who uses a food processor still knows how to use a knife. The tool makes them faster. The skill makes them better.
